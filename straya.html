An American's journey immigrating to Australia

Hi, my name is James.

On June 25th 2017, I left San Francisco and moved to Sydney.

The following is a log of everything I learned while living in Australia.

June 27th, 2017

Pizza comes in much smaller sizes here for about the same price– but all Coke is Mexi-Coke which more than makes up for it.

June 28th, 2017

Australians are very proud of their coffee– which is not that special, telling them this upsets them greatly.

June 29th, 2017

I held up a line at the Opal "top-up" thing today cause I didn't know what "cheque" meant.

Still have not seen a kangaroo.

June 30th, 2017

The have this thing called "Fairy bread" which is made up of cheap white bread, butter, and jimmies. It looks disgusting, but this seems to be a hill they are willing to die on.

July 1st, 2017

"Chips" is a stupid name. They're called french fries damnit. If anyone owns the naming rights to fried food, it's America.

July 2nd, 2017

I was afraid to order at the Woolworth's Deli. I wasn't sure if I should ask for 250 grams or a quarter kilo of turkey. Both sounded weird to me.

They also pre-cut the meats and cheeses and just stuff them into a bag. So in case you were worried that your cheese would be fresh, they've made sure to air it out for you.
